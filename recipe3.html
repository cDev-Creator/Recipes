Miso Soup with Buckwheat Soba Noodles

Description

A wholesome vegan miso soup filled with vegetables and noodles. It’s the perfect recipe for cooler weather, so soothing and easy to put together! The most delicious, quick and easy, immune boosting bowl of noodle soup ever!

Miso soup is a great addition to a healthy diet. It can be filled with a variety of veggies which is an important part of a wholesome meal. Miso is also fermented and very soothing to your belly and provides your digestive system with good bacteria.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil optional, can use water
  • 1 teaspoon chopped garlic
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon red miso paste (if you don't have red use another tablespoon of white)
  • 5 ounces mushrooms, sliced (any kind or a mix, I used shiitake)
  • 1 sliced carrot
  • 5 ounces baby bok choy, chopped (about 1 heads)
  • 1 ounce green onions, sliced (about 2)
  • 1 bundle of Korean Buckwheat Soba noodles
  • ½ cup diced extra firm tofu, optional
  • 8 ounces spinach, optional

Instructions

  1. Saute garlic in sesame oil or water in a soup pot over medium heat until soft, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Mix 1/2 cup of the water (make sure it is warm) with the miso paste until the miso is dissolved. Set aside.
  3. Add the mushrooms, carrots, bok choy, and onions into the pot. Cook until the veggies are softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the noodles into a seprate pot and cook accoding to package direcrions. Set aside once done.
  5. Add the rest of the water and tofu, if using. Bring to a boil, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add in the water miso mix, noodles and spinach, if using. Cook until spinach is wilted, about 2 minutes. Serve hot!
